# CLYR Community Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We want a community where people can examine risky ideas candidly without attacking one another. Be respectful, specific, patient, and evidence-led. Welcome different levels of Windows, security, accessibility, product, and testing experience. Critique behavior and designs, not people.

Examples of constructive participation include:

- explaining safety or privacy impact with a reproducible synthetic example;
- acknowledging uncertainty and correcting claims when evidence changes;
- preserving contributor attribution and unrelated work;
- making reviews accessible, actionable, and proportionate to risk;
- stopping a potentially unsafe test and asking for review.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, publishing private paths or data, encouraging destructive tests on real machines, evading project safety controls, or retaliating against a good-faith security report.

## Scope

This code applies in project repositories, reviews, issue trackers, discussions, events, and private communication made in an official project role. It also applies when someone publicly represents CLYR.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to repository maintainers through a private channel they publish. If no safe channel exists, open a non-sensitive request for one; do not disclose personal or security details publicly. Maintainers with a conflict of interest must recuse themselves.

Maintainers may clarify expectations, edit or remove harmful content, issue a warning, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently remove a participant. Responses should consider impact, pattern, intent, repair, and community safety. Report details are shared only with people needed to handle the case, subject to legal/safety duties.

No person should face retaliation for a good-faith report. Knowingly false reports and weaponized process are also violations.
